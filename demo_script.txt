PREPARED ANSWERS FOR JUDGE QUESTIONS
================================================================

Q: "Why T001 for energy calibration?"
A: "T001 is the only batch with complete time-series sensor
   data across all 8 manufacturing phases. We extracted
   phase-level energy proportions from it and used those
   as calibration coefficients for all 60 batches. The
   estimates are validated — energy range of 58 to 91 kWh
   is physically realistic for this process."

Q: "What prevents the operator from always rejecting?"
A: "All decisions are logged with timestamps and reasons.
   The HITL audit log creates accountability. In a production
   deployment, rejection patterns would be visible to
   plant managers. The system learns from rejections too —
   they inform future weight adjustments."

Q: "How does the GS update without human approval?"
A: "It does not. The Pareto dominance check identifies
   candidates, but the update only executes after the operator
   clicks ACCEPT. This is a design choice — we enforce human
   oversight on all Golden Signature changes."

Q: "What is the carbon emission factor?"
A: "0.000233 kg CO2 per Wh — the India grid average emission
   factor. This converts our energy estimates to carbon
   footprint, allowing us to track sustainability targets
   against real-world benchmarks."

Q: "Can this generalize to other manufacturing domains?"
A: "Yes. The process parameter columns, quality output columns,
   and objective weights are all defined in config.py.
   Changing the domain requires only updating that single
   configuration file and reloading the dataset."

Q: "What does Pareto-optimal mean here practically?"
A: "A batch is Pareto-optimal if no other batch beats it
   on all five objectives at the same time. 21 of our 60
   batches are on this frontier. These are the only batches
   worth considering as Golden Signature candidates —
   any other batch is provably suboptimal."
